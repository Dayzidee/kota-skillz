# 5 Web Design Directions

To avoid building sloppy or generic websites, designers can look to established art and design movements. Here are five distinct design directions to learn and apply:

## 1. Swiss Style
*   **Characteristics:** Focuses on cleanliness, readability, and objectivity. Features asymmetric layouts, sans-serif typography, and strict adherence to grid systems.

## 2. Bauhaus
*   **Characteristics:** Merges function with aesthetics. Recognizable by its use of primary colors, bold geometric shapes, and functional, uncluttered layouts.

## 3. Brutalism
*   **Characteristics:** Raw, unpolished, and structurally exposed. Often features harsh contrasts, oversized typography, and a deliberate rejection of traditional, comfortable aesthetics.

## 4. Editorial
*   **Characteristics:** Treats the web canvas like a high-end publication. Relies on sophisticated typography, ample whitespace, and high-quality photography carefully arranged in columns.

## 5. Organic
*   **Characteristics:** Inspired by nature. Utilizes earthy color palettes, fluid shapes, soft gradients, and natural textures to create a calming, approachable interface.
