# Creative Ways to Apply Brand Colors

Applying brand colors doesn't just mean changing the font or button color. Here are five creative ways to integrate a color palette into a website's UI:

## 1. Color as a Product Background
*   **Application:** Take a color from the brand palette and apply it as a solid fill or a smooth gradient directly behind the product image.
*   **Effect:** Makes the product pop while anchoring it to the brand identity.

## 2. Gradients on Headings
*   **Application:** Apply the brand color as a gradient mask specifically to the large heading typography.
*   **Effect:** Adds visual interest to text-heavy sections without overwhelming the page.

## 3. Color per Category
*   **Application:** When presenting multiple offers, features, or options, assign each item its own specific color from the brand palette.
*   **Effect:** Helps users visually separate and categorize different pieces of information.

## 4. Color on a Grid
*   **Application:** In a grid layout, give each card or feature block a different tint or shade derived from the main color palette.
*   **Effect:** Creates a dynamic, mosaic-like structure that remains cohesive.

## 5. One Tint Across Layout
*   **Application:** Bathe an entire section in a single color tone.
*   **Effect:** Unifies the imagery, text, and background, making the design feel highly deliberate and considerate.
