# Hero Section Design Styles

Not every hero section should follow the standard layout. Exploring different compositional layouts can completely change the feel of a website. Here are six distinct layout directions for hero sections:

## 1. Split
*   **Layout:** Divides the hero section into distinct halves (often vertically), separating text content from visual imagery.

## 2. Annotated
*   **Layout:** Uses floating text, tooltips, or small descriptive elements pointing to specific parts of the main image or typography to guide the user's eye.

## 3. Editorial
*   **Layout:** Mimics print editorial design, focusing on strong typography, varying font weights, and structured text columns over a clean background.

## 4. Illustrative
*   **Layout:** Relies heavily on custom illustrations and abstract shapes as the primary visual draw rather than standard photography.

## 5. Magazine
*   **Layout:** Uses bold, oversized typography paired with overlapping cutout photography to emulate a modern magazine cover.

## 6. Centered
*   **Layout:** A minimalist approach where the headline, subheadline, and call-to-action are perfectly center-aligned with abundant whitespace.
